Things to do in Sri Lanka

Experiences

Journey Beyond Boundaries

Gangaramaya Temple

A mix of modern architecture and cultural significance, the complex comprises the main temple, a museum, a library and a vocational training centre.

Colombo National Museum

A beautiful colonial building surrounded by vast grounds, a collection of relics, artefacts and historical paraphernalia from Sri Lanka’s antiquity are housed here.

Independence Square

A national monument of Kandyan style architecture, this landmark and the surrounding square was built in 1948 to commemorate Independence from British rule.

Waterfalls Trek

Sinharaja reserve is home to 19 waterfalls. This trek will take you through the forest to admire the magnificence of some of them.

Rope Swings at Dalawella and Wijeya Beach

Become insta-famous with your own photos at these popular spots. The views will grab you even if rope swinging is not your thing.

Japanese Peace Pagoda Rumassala

Built in 2005 as a symbol of peace, this unique shrine on Rumassala hill also offers incredible views of the coastline and Galle.

Relax at Jungle Beach

One of the most famous and secluded beaches in Unawatuna, this pristine little cove flanked by jungle is an ideal place to chill.

Cultural Dance Show

Swirls of vibrant colour and pulsating rhythms; dances and drums that keep traditions of old alive. Witness an unforgettable display of Kandyan heritage.

Peradeniya Botanical Garden

Sprawled across 147 acres, the garden includes more than 4000 species of plants and trees, including rare orchids, spices, medicinal herbs and palms.

Temple of the Tooth Relic

Revered by pilgrims from around the world, the ‘Sri Dalada Maligawa’ is a sacred Buddhist shrine housing a tooth relic of Lord Buddha.

Sithulpawwa Buddhist Monastery

On a rock rising above the wilderness, this ancient Buddhist monastery and cave - dating back to 2nd century BC - offers serenity and views.

Bundala Game Drive

A must for birdwatchers - over 150 species of birds are found here at any time of the year, as well as other wildlife.

Yala Game Drive

Take a morning or evening game drive to meet Sri Lanka’s big five: leopard, sloth bear, elephant, buffalo, crocodiles, and many others too.

Safari Camping

Want to experience the jungle more intimately? There’s perhaps nothing more thrilling than camping, even if you choose to do so in luxury.

Ellawala Waterfall

Trek or cycle through jungle and fields to this gentle cascade where you can cool off and just feel at one with nature.

Waterfall Trek

At 220 metres high, Diyaluma is Sri Lanka’s 2nd highest waterfall, and Uda Diyaluma is its upper segment with the insta-famous bathing pool.

Archaeology Experience

Visit the historical sites of Buduruwagala, Yudaganawa and Maligawila where giant Buddha statues and rock carvings from centuries ago have survived relatively unscathed.

Snorkelling and Diving

Slip into the shallow reefs teeming with marine life, or explore popular dive sites including Prince Heinrich Patch, Bridge Rock and Yala Rock.
